Imaging Guidelines

Follow these guidelines to achieve optimal automated screening results.

🎯
Center Position
The circular microscope field-of-view must be fully within the frame and centered. Misaligned or cut-off fields will reduce detection accuracy.
💡
Even Illumination
Avoid shadows and vignetting especially along the image border. Ensure consistent, uniform illumination across the entire field.
🔍
Clean Lens
Clean both the eyepiece and phone camera lens before each session. Smudges or marks on the lens will be mistaken for cellular structures.
🦠
Cell Distribution
Aim for well-separated single cells when possible. Dense cell clusters prevent accurate segmentation; sparse fields reduce counting efficiency.
📐
Sharp Focus
Each captured image must be in sharp focus with no motion blur. Wait for lens stabilization before each capture in the camera view.
📱
Recommended Hardware
Samsung Galaxy S Series (12+ MP, single-lens). Microscope adapter from telescopeadapters.com/128-universal-mounts. 100× objective lens.
✅ Good vs ❌ Bad — Quick Reference
✅ Correct Imaging
❌ Avoid This
✅
Centered, full field
Circular FOV fully visible and centered in the frame.
❌
Off-center or cut-off
FOV too small, misaligned, or extends beyond image edges.
✅
Uniform illumination
Consistent brightness across the entire field with no border shadows.
❌
Shadow/vignetting
Dark edges from misalignment, dirty lens or poor lighting conditions.
✅
Well-separated cells
Individual cells are distinguishable, not clumped together.
❌
Too dense or too sparse
Overlapping cell clusters or very few cells per field impair accuracy.
✅
Sharp, in-focus image
Crisp cell boundaries with no motion blur or out-of-focus blur.
❌
Blurry or smeared
Motion during capture or incorrect focus position causes blur artifacts.
